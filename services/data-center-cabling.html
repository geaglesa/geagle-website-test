Data Center Cabling in Saudi Arabia

A Data Center Is Only as Strong as the Cabling Behind It

The most powerful servers and networking equipment will underperform if the cabling behind them is poorly planned, incorrectly installed, or badly managed. Geagle designs and installs professional data center cabling infrastructure that is clean, structured, standards-compliant, built to perform, and ready to grow with your business.

Overview

Purpose-built technology for Saudi businesses

Inside every data center, from purpose-built facilities to corporate server rooms, the physical layer supports everything. Structured cabling, patch panels, cable trays, racks, power distribution, and physical organization affect network performance, troubleshooting time, upgrade costs, and downtime. Geagle has delivered data center cabling projects across corporate offices, government facilities, hospitals, industrial sites, and enterprise data centers throughout Saudi Arabia, following TIA-942, ISO/IEC 11801, and BICSI guidelines.

FAQ

Common questions

QWhat cabling standards do you follow?+

We follow international standards including TIA-942 for data center infrastructure, ISO/IEC 11801 for structured cabling, and BICSI installation best practices.

QWhat is the difference between Cat6 and Cat6A cabling?+

Cat6 supports 10 Gigabit Ethernet up to 55 meters. Cat6A supports 10 Gigabit Ethernet up to 100 meters with better shielding and less interference, making it preferred for many data center environments.

QCan you clean up and reorganize our existing data center cabling?+

Yes. We audit existing infrastructure, remove redundant cabling, reorganize and re-label connections, and produce updated documentation for a cleaner environment.

QDo you test and certify all cabling after installation?+

Yes. Copper runs are tested with cable certifiers, and fiber runs are tested with OTDR and optical power meters. Full test reports are included in the handover pack.

QHow do you manage cabling projects without disrupting live data center operations?+

We phase installations, work around live equipment, and schedule disruptive work during maintenance windows, with clear communication throughout the project.
